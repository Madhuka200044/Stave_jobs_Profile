founder of apple

Steven Paul Jobs (February 24, 1955 – October 5, 2011) was an American businessman, inventor, and investor best known for co-founding the technology company Apple Inc. Jobs was also the founder of NeXT and chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar. He was a pioneer of the personal computer revolution of the 1970s and 1980s, along with his early business partner and fellow Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak. Jobs was born in San Francisco in 1955 and adopted shortly afterwards. He attended Reed College in 1972 before withdrawing that same year. In 1974, he traveled through India, seeking enlightenment before later studying Zen Buddhism. He and Wozniak co-founded Apple in 1976 to further develop and sell Wozniak's Apple I personal computer. Together, the duo gained fame and wealth a year later with production and sale of the Apple II, one of the first highly successful mass-produced microcomputers. Jobs saw the commercial potential of the Xerox Alto in 1979, which was mouse-driven and had a graphical user interface (GUI). This led to the development of the unsuccessful Apple Lisa in 1983, followed by the breakthrough Macintosh in 1984, the first mass-produced computer with a GUI. The Macintosh launched the desktop publishing industry in 1985 with the addition of the Apple LaserWriter, the first laser printer to feature vector graphics and PostScript.

The folowing list is the timeline of steve's life

  • 1955-Steve is born on February 24th in San Francisco, California.
  • 1967-Moves to Los Altos, California, and begins to show interest in electronics.
  • 1968-Meets future business partner, Steve Wozniak, through a mutual friend.
  • 1972-Graduates from Homestead High School in Cupertino, California.Enrolls at Reed College in Portland, Oregon, but drops out after one semester.
  • 1974-Takes a job at Atari, a video game company, and travels to India for spiritual enlightenment
  • 1976-Co-founds Apple Computer, Inc. with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne in his parents’ garage.
  • 1977-Apple releases the Apple II, one of the first highly successful mass-produced microcomputers
  • 1980-Apple goes public, making Steve a millionaire.
  • 1983-Lures John Sculley from PepsiCo to become Apple's CEO.
  • 1984-Launches the Macintosh, a revolutionary personal computer with a graphical user interface
  • 1985-Faces a power struggle with John Sculley and is ousted from Apple.
  • 1986-Starts NeXT Inc., a computer platform development company targeting higher education and business markets.
  • 1991-Acquires The Graphics Group, which later becomes Pixar Animation Studios.
  • 1996-Pixar strikes a deal with Disney to produce animated feature films.
  • 1997-Introduces the iPod, revolutionizing the music industry.
  • 2001-Undergoes surgery for a rare form of pancreatic cancer.
  • 2004-Disney acquires Pixar, making Steve the largest single shareholder of Disney.
  • 2006-Introduces the iPhone, changing the mobile phone industry.
  • 2007-Launches the App Store, creating a new economy for software developers.
  • 2010-Introduces the iPad, defining a new category of portable computing devices.
  • 2011-Steps down as CEO of Apple due to health issues.
  • 2011-Passes away on October 5th, leaving a profound impact on technology and design.
